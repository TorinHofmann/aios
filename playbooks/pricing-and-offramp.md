# Pricing + Off-Ramp

## Anchor

**$6K/week, weekly off-ramp.**

- ~$187/hr blended for ~32 hrs/week (3 days on-site + 2 days remote research + prompt design)
- Cadence: Mon/Wed/Fri on-site, Tue/Thu remote
- Friday EOD = natural review point. "If the week was worthwhile, week 2 happens."

## Trajectories

| Length | Total | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| 4 weeks | $24K | Standard first engagement. 4-6 agents shipped + team trained. |
| 6 weeks | $36K | First engagement extends + early case study compounding. |
| 8 weeks | $48K | Phase 1 + early Phase 2 (content engine, cohort assistant, etc.) |
| 12 weeks | $72K | Long-arc engagement with parallel content / outbound work. |

## Pre-pay levers

- 4 weeks pre-paid: 5% off ($22.8K)
- 6 weeks pre-paid: 7% off ($33.5K)
- Use these only when the client asks for a discount; never lead with them.

## Floor + walk

- **Floor:** $5K/week WITH scope cut (drop Friday on-site → Mon/Wed only)
- **Walk:** $4.5K. Below this you're under-priced for the value and the engagement loses energy.

## Below-market for first big clients

For the first 2-3 clients, lean below market. Case study + referrals matter more than max cash early. Prices increase after 2-3 strong references. Don't offer the discount — anchor at $6K and let them have it because they're the first.

## Non-negotiables (in every contract)

- 15% pro-referral commission on any client they refer who books
- Case study + co-marketing rights
- Work-for-hire IP: client owns what's built; you keep generic skills + integrations + playbooks
- Client's own Anthropic account (not yours)
- Mutual NDA
- **NO equity** in operating companies (mortgage, dental, e-com, etc — thin margins for outside operators)

## Why weekly off-ramp wins

- Lowers commitment friction — easier to say yes to "$6K and see how week 1 goes" than "$24K and trust me"
- Creates accountability rhythm — every Friday you have to defend the next week
- Filters bad-fit clients early — if they're not seeing value at week 1 you both walk before $24K is on the line
- Produces case-study moments at every Friday checkpoint, not just at the end

## Confidence anchor

Match price to defensible confidence. If you have N successful engagements, your price defends itself. If you have 0-1, anchor at hour-math you can show your work on, not market comps a 10-year specialist would charge. Underpricing the first client is fine; overpricing them is fatal.
