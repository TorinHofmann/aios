# The 20% Productivity Dip Warning

**Frame this Day 1 with the owner — before any team onboarding.** Without the warning, the dip causes panic. With it, everyone stays the course.

## The script

> "Days 2-5 your team's productivity may dip ~20%. They're learning a new tool while doing their job — context switching, getting Claude to do what they want, breaking and fixing prompts. It's friction. By end of Week 2 they're 20%+ faster than baseline. This is normal and worth it — but I want you to know it's coming so nobody bails mid-engagement when it feels worse before it gets better."

## Why it matters

- New tools always cause a J-curve. The dip is real.
- If the owner hears "20% slower this week" without warning, they assume the engagement isn't working.
- The team senses owner panic and disengages.
- Stating it upfront frames the dip as **expected progress**, not failure.

## When to repeat the warning

- Day 1 morning, owner only (set the expectation)
- Day 2 lunch, team-wide (you'll feel it tomorrow, here's why)
- Friday EOD if anyone is grumbling (the dip is real, the recovery is in)

## Counter-signals to watch for

- Team member quits using Claude after Day 2 — they hit friction and gave up. Pair with them, find an easier first task.
- Owner asks "is this working?" Day 3 — the dip is hitting them. Reassure + show one concrete time-savings example.
- Team member doubles-down on Claude Day 3-4 — leading indicator they're past the dip. Surface this win to the owner.

## What the recovery looks like

- Day 5: team member can run 1-2 things on Claude without help
- Week 2 Day 1: that team member trains another team member while you watch
- Week 2 Day 5: owner notices something shipped faster than usual and connects it to the engagement

If you don't see those markers, something's wrong with the onboarding pace, not the tool.
