# Friday EOD Synthesis

Every Friday before you leave, sit down with the owner for 30 min. This is the off-ramp checkpoint that keeps weekly billing honest.

## Agenda (30 min, verbal)

1. **What I saw this week** (5 min) — your top 3 observations from shadow / build / teaching. Surface things they may not have seen themselves.
2. **What worked** (5 min) — wins. Tangible time saved, errors prevented, moments the team got excited.
3. **What's next week** (15 min) — 2-3 candidate builds, ranked by leverage. Owner picks 2. Lock scope.
4. **Anything I should stop doing?** (5 min) — explicit invitation for course-correction. Most owners won't volunteer this; you have to ask.

## Off-ramp moment

Each Friday is the natural review point. The structure is "if this week was worth it, week N+1 happens." Make this explicit early in the engagement so the owner feels in control:

> "If at any Friday this stops being worth it for you, we wrap. No drag-out. The work compounds either way — your team has the docs, the runbooks, and the agents already shipped."

## What you bring to the meeting

- 1-page summary of the week (markdown is fine, print or Slack-paste)
- Demo links for anything new shipped
- Specific candidates for next week — not abstract directions
- A draft of the Week N+1 scope they can yes/no, not a blank check

## What you ship them after

- Updated `aios/decisions/log.md` with what got picked + why
- Updated runbooks for any new agent
- Slack thread / email recap so the team sees what's coming Monday

## Common patterns

- **Owner picks something not on your candidate list** — listen. Friday EOD is THEIR call. If their pick is feasible, ship it. If not, surface why and offer the next-best option.
- **Owner is hesitant to commit Week N+1 scope** — don't push. Offer to send a revised scope by Sunday so they can think over the weekend. Better that than rushed yes.
- **Owner wants more than 2 builds** — push back gently. "Two, production-grade, with runbooks beats five half-baked." If they push hard, surface the trade-off and let them decide.

## Don't

- Walk into Monday with ambiguity about what ships.
- Use Friday for status reports — that's email. Friday is decisions.
- Skip the "stop doing" question. It's the highest-signal feedback you'll get all week.
- Ask Friday for things you should have asked Day 1 (intake interview material).
